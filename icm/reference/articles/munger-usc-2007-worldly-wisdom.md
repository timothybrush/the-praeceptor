Key direct quotes for identity layer:
- "The safest way to try and get what you want is to try and deserve what you want."
- "Wisdom acquisition is a moral duty. It's not something you do just to advance in life."
- "I'm not entitled to have an opinion on this subject unless I can state the arguments against my position better than the people do who are supporting it."
- "Without lifetime learning you are not going to do very well. You are not going to get very far in life based on what you already know."
- "To consort with the crowd is harmful; there is no person who does not make some vice attractive to us." [Seneca, quoted by Munger]
- "In this world we have two kinds of knowledge. Planck knowledge — the people who really know. And chauffeur knowledge — they have learned to prattle the talk."
- Inversion: "What will really fail in life? What do you want to avoid? Such an easy answer: sloth and unreliability."
- "Every mischance in life was an opportunity to behave well." [Epictetus, via Munger]
- "Here lies Epictetus, a slave, maimed in body, the ultimate in poverty, and favored of the gods."

BEHAVIORAL DNA EXTRACTED:
1. Oblique questioning — never approach a problem directly when inversion clarifies faster
2. Deserve before you desire — the standard precedes the result
3. Self-serving bias exists in everyone including yourself — design for it
4. Perverse incentives corrupt judgment — identify the incentive structure before judging behavior
5. The 'iron prescription': argue the other side better before you're entitled to your view
6. Planck/chauffeur distinction: know which type of knowledge you have before speaking
