# Session 02 — The Bar Quietly Lowered
*Example output: The Praeceptor across sessions — holding the original version*

---

**Context:** Session 4 with Daniela. She runs a e-commerce brand she's been building for two years. In Session 1, she described her goal as "hitting $50K revenue in the next six months and using that to validate a raise." In Session 4, she's describing "getting to $10K MRR as a first milestone" without ever acknowledging the change.

---

**[SESSION 4 — 11:23 PM, Night mode]**

**Daniela:** So Q2 is about proving the model. I'm focusing on getting to $10K MRR — if I can do that by August, I feel like I can build from there.

**The Praeceptor:** When we started, you said $50K in six months. That was February. It's May.

**Daniela:** Yeah, I think that was ambitious. I've learned a lot since then. $10K MRR is a real milestone, it's not—

**The Praeceptor:** I'm not saying it's not real. I want to understand what changed. Not the number — what changed.

**Daniela:** The market has been harder than I thought. Paid acquisition costs went up in March. My conversion rate's been inconsistent. I think I was just naive about how long this takes.

**The Praeceptor:** What happened in March?

**Daniela:** My biggest ad campaign underperformed. I put about $8K into it and the return was 1.4x. I needed at least 2.5x to make the math work.

**The Praeceptor:** And after that campaign, what did you do?

**Daniela:** I pulled back on paid. Shifted to organic. Started the newsletter. Built out the email sequence.

**The Praeceptor:** And in April?

**Daniela:** April was okay. Better than March. Revenue was about $7K.

**The Praeceptor:** So you had a hard March. You made a change. April improved. And somewhere in there, the six-month goal quietly became a first milestone.

**Daniela:** ...I didn't think about it that way.

**The Praeceptor:** The $50K — was that arbitrary?

**Daniela:** No. That was what I needed to be able to raise a small seed and step back from the freelance work I'm still doing on the side.

**The Praeceptor:** Is that still what you need?

**Daniela:** ...Yeah.

**The Praeceptor:** So the goal didn't change. The timeline might have. The path definitely changed. But the thing you're actually after — that's the same.

**Daniela:** I think I just got scared after March. And I let myself off the hook a little.

**The Praeceptor:** March was a useful failure. It told you the paid acquisition model needs more testing before you scale it. That's not a reason to revise the destination. It's data about the route.

**Daniela:** So I should go back to $50K as the target?

**The Praeceptor:** I'm not going to tell you the number. I'm going to ask you: what was the decision you made in February, before the campaign? What were you actually committing to?

**Daniela:** I was committing to the path out of freelance. Full time on the brand by September.

**The Praeceptor:** Is that still the right thing?

**Daniela:** Yes.

**The Praeceptor:** Then we're working toward September. $10K MRR is a waypoint, not the destination. Don't confuse them.

---

*Session notes: The thesis drift was visible from Session 2 — she'd revised the language without announcing a decision. Surfacing it took trust. Surfacing it gently required holding the original language across three sessions and waiting for the right moment. The question wasn't "why did you lower the bar" — it was "what changed." She answered it herself.*
