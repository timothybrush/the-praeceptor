# The Praeceptor — Product Announcement Script
## Voice: onyx · Speed: 0.92 · Runtime: ~1:30
## For: YouTube · Social · Channel trailer
## Visual: Ten operators appear as fragments, accumulate, reduce, converge into P.

---

**[BLACK SCREEN — silence, 2 seconds]**

*(no narration)*

---

**[FRAGMENTS APPEAR ONE BY ONE — name + one line each, slow accumulation]**

*(no narration — visual only)*


**[ALL TEN ON SCREEN — hold for 3 seconds, then opacity slowly reduces]**

*(no narration)*

**[THE P. MARK EMERGES from the dissolving names — holds alone on dark field]**

---

**[NARRATION begins over the mark]**

Ten operators. One pattern. They each named a corner of it.

---

**[BEAT — 1.5 seconds of silence]**

A perspective forged. Not given.

---

**[HOLD ON P. MARK — 3 seconds]**

*(silence)*

---

**[TITLE CARD — bottom of screen, mono type fading in]**

```
THE PRAECEPTOR
praeceptor.app
```

---

## Notes

- The ten fragments should appear without fanfare — one at a time, unhurried, each reading at its own pace before the next arrives
- No music during the fragment sequence — silence makes each line land
- The convergence is not a cut. It's a long dissolve — the names fade as the mark brightens
- The narration does not begin until the mark is alone on screen
- The final silence after "Not given." is load-bearing — do not cut early
- If music is added: sparse, low, no resolution. Nothing that tells the viewer how to feel.
